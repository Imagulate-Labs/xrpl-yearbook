The XRP Yearbook's financial layer launches on one principle: **fairness you can verify — not fairness you're asked to trust.**

The market is full of people who *want* to put money into something, and a graveyard of reasons they won't: devs who run, teams that dump, bots that snipe the open before a real person gets a fill, wash traders faking volume. Every one of those is theft. The opening nobody is taking: **be the launch where the buyer gets a receipt proving they weren't robbed.**

That's the product. Fairness is the feature.

## The four thefts — and how we provably prevent each

A fair launch isn't a promise. It's a set of on-chain facts anyone can check.

1. **The Rug** — dev pulls liquidity and runs.
   → Liquidity **locked on-chain for a fixed term.** Anyone can verify the lock and its expiry.
2. **The Dump** — team sells on its own holders.
   → Team/treasury allocation **disclosed up front and time-locked / vested**, lock verifiable. No hidden bags, no surprise unlocks.
3. **The Snipe** — bots front-run the open.
   → **Fair open:** every real participant gets an equal shot, no pre-mine advantage, no bot tax. (Exact mechanic — fair claim window vs. equal-price open — is a team decision.)
4. **The Wash** — fake volume to bait buyers.
   → **Transparent, on-chain activity**, no self-dealing volume. What you see is real.

Each one ships with its receipt. That bundle is **Proof of Fair Launch.**

Every gate green → the buyer gets a receipt. One gate red → no badge. **The certificate is the product.**
